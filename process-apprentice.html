The Process

Application

In order to be accepted into the Apprenticeship Program, you must first fill out a short form indicating your experience level and previous work. Don't be nervous - we want to balance our senior developers with interested newbie coders, so please give it a shot! Whether you've been coding for years or just graduated from Codecademy, there's a place for you here.

Working on Sites

You will usually be paired in teams of 3-4 developers on a site. You'll need to create a schedule, collaborate with others, and work as a team. Your work directly reflects on the Apprenticeship Program, so please make sure to hit all your deadlines and communicate well with your client!

Professional Development

Right off the bat, you'll be given space for a personal site that you can use for anything - a resume, a new webapp, etc. You'll also make contacts in the industry and kickstart your career. One of the best things about being in the Apprenticeship Program is access to our network of senior developers, many of whom have won hackathons, interned at tech startups, and start their own companies. Ask them how they got their start and what resources they used! In the future, we'll be adding even more tools for you to use, like a subscription to Parse or Firebase. You might even end up working at Synapps yourself!

Community Service

As an unpaid intern, all the time you spend on the website counts as community service. If you want community service, we will definitely be able to sign off on whatever hours you need. Contact us for more details as needed!
